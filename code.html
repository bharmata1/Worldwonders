The New Seven Wonders of the World

Chichen Itza

Chichen Itza was a large city built by the Mayan civilization in the early 400s A.D.

It is located on the Yucatan Peninsula in Mexico and is considered to be one of the most influential cities ever.

It is one of the most visited locations in Mexico with several million tourists visiting each year.

Christ Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer was constructed between 1922 and 1931. It is located in Rio De Janeiro, Brazil and was built overlooking the city.

The statue is nearly 100 feet high and his arms are stretched 92 feet wide. It has a weight of 635 metric tons.

The Great Wall of China

The Great wall of China was a series of fortifications built in order to prevent attacks against nomadic groups.

Constrcution began in the 7th century BC and concluded in 1878. It streches over 21,000 kilometers.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu was built by the Inca civilization in the 15th century in southern Peru.

It was constructed atop the Andean mountain range approximately 7,500 feet above sea level.

Petra

Petra is a historical city in southern Jordan. Construction of this great city was during the 4th century B.C.

The city is a hotspot for tourists resulting in over one million people visiting in 2019.

Because of the COVID19 pandemic, no one has visited the city since March 2020.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal was commissioned in 1632 by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan. It's purpose was to be the tomb of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal.

It is located in Agra, India and has millions of tourists visit each year.

The Roman Colosseum

The Colosseum construction began in 72 CE under Emperor Vespasian and was completed in 80 CE under Emperor Titus.

It had many purposes, the most common being for gladiator contests, public spectacles, famous battle reenactments, and public executions.
